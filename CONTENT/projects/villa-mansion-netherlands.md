## Case Study Narrative

### The Brief

[PENDING — 2–3 sentences about what the client wanted. Example framework:]

The clients approached By Shakir with a newly built villa and a clear ambition: an interior that felt warm and collected, not showroom-new. The architecture provided generous proportions and abundant natural light. The design brief was to complement those qualities without competing with them.

### The Approach

[PENDING — adapt with real project details. Framework:]

The material palette took its cues from the surrounding landscape. Natural stone flooring, warm timber joinery and linen-upholstered furnishings create a layered, inhabitable quality throughout. Every room connects to the next through a consistent material language, while each space retains its own character.

The living areas open onto the garden through full-height glazing. Furniture was positioned to frame the view rather than dominate it. In the evening, layered lighting transforms the same spaces into something quieter and more intimate.

### The Detail

Bespoke joinery runs through the entire villa: the kitchen cabinetry, a built-in media wall in the living room and full-height wardrobes in the primary bedroom. Each piece was designed in [3D](/services/3d-design/) and approved by the client before manufacture began.
