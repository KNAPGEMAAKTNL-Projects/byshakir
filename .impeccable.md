## Design Context

### Users

**Who they are:** High-net-worth private clients commissioning luxury interior design. Projects start at ~€50,000 and include complete home interiors — floors, walls, furniture, styling. International reach: Netherlands, Dubai, Spain, Morocco.

**Their mindset when they arrive:** "I'm planning a major project and comparing quality." Decision timeline is weeks to months. They browse slowly, looking at the portfolio deeply before making any contact.

**What they are NOT:** Price-shoppers who want to scan quickly, check pricing, and move on. The site must make them feel before they think. Those who won't invest time browsing are not the target client.

**Job to be done:** Find a designer whose aesthetic resonates with their vision and who they feel comfortable trusting with their home. Portfolio depth and personal connection are the primary trust signals — not certifications or metrics.

**Context of use:** ~50% desktop/tablet, ~50% mobile. Desktop/tablet users are the primary decision-making audience; they browse in a relaxed, considered state, not under urgency. No emergency triggers, no time pressure.

---

### Brand Personality

**Three words (client's own):** Warm · Approachable · Exotic

**Extended personality:** The brand is a person you'd want to have coffee with — not a cold magazine spread. The "exotic" quality comes from the international project scope (Dubai, Morocco, Barcelona) and By Shakir's Mediterranean/Middle Eastern aesthetic sensibility. This sets the brand apart in a Dutch market that skews toward minimalist Scandinavian interiors.

**Tone of voice:** Personal, warm, unhurried. Uses "je" (not "u") — informal Dutch address. Never aggressive sales language. Never urgency language. The brand speaks like a trusted expert who knows their work speaks for itself.

**Emotional goal (Q4 answer, verbatim):** "This feels inviting, cozy, clean."
- Inviting: the site should feel welcoming, not intimidating
- Cozy: warm, human-scaled, not cold museum luxury
- Clean: disciplined, nothing extraneous, visual breathing room

**Spatial metaphor (Q3, verbatim):** "Feeling the warm breeze while looking over a city like Dubai standing in the terrace of a penthouse in the shadow. Mediterranean exotic pool to cool down whenever, but for now enjoying the breeze."

This metaphor is the design anchor. Everything — palette, typography, motion, layout — should evoke a shaded penthouse terrace: warm but not harsh, rich but liveable, ordered but expansive.

### Aesthetic Direction

**Visual tone:** Warm Editorial Luxury. Not cold Scandinavian minimalism, not brash gold-and-marble excess. The "shaded terracotta" direction — the warmth of Mediterranean stone at rest.

**Tone scores:** Energy 4 · Density 3 · Formality 7 · Richness 10 · Animation 6

**References (approved competitors, from research):**
- **Piet Boon** (pietboon.com) — smooth, great visuals, very clean. "Feels smooth, great visuals, very clean."
- **Best Interiors** (bestinteriors.nl) — most modern website, super clean, great project display. "Website van het jaar 2025."
- **Kolenik Design** (kolenikdesign.com) — endless project gallery, very pretty project pages, nice project detail display.
- **Lagrand Interior Design** (en.lagrand.nl) — well curated and arty, very pretty hero, clear loading state between page switches "making it feel high end."

**Anti-references (rejected, from research + Q5):**
- **Lux Interieurbouw (4/10):** Old animations, commercial carousel, copper gradient buttons, metrics-as-trust-signals ("234 projecten")
- **Set Interieur (4/10):** Doesn't use screen maximum, monochromatic, not portfolio-forward enough
- **Laura Hindriks (5/10):** "Not using screen maximum, it's cropped to the middle, which feels weird on PC"
- **Eric Kuster (7/10):** "Very arty website, it just lacks basic functionality — links to booking page without an actual way to book anything"

**Typography:**
- Heading: Cormorant Garamond — weights 300 (hero) / 400 (H2-H3) / 700 (emphasis). Variable font (wght axis). Old Style Garalde, warm, high contrast, wide letterforms.
- Body: DM Sans — weights 300 / 400 / 500. Variable font (opsz + wght axes). Not on overused list. Quality Tier A.
- Navigation: DM Sans, ALL CAPS, `letter-spacing: 0.15em`, weight 500
- Scale: perfect-fourth (1.333). Display hero uses `clamp(3rem, 2rem + 5vw, 7rem)`.
- Hero headings at weight 300 — the "expensive light" luxury signal.
- NEVER weight 600-800 on headings. That's the trades/construction register.

**Icon system:** NONE. F7 + R10 meets "Formality 7+ AND Richness 8+" threshold for skipping icons entirely. Three inline SVGs for UI indicators (arrow, menu, close) — no library.

**Motion philosophy:** "Warm Breeze" — slow, gentle, deliberate.
- Easing: `cubic-bezier(0.83, 0, 0.17, 1)` (Dramatic) — applied as `--ease-project` globally
- Duration baseline: 1.4s reveals / 0.7s hover / 1.0s page transitions
- Scroll: scale-reveal (0.95→1, 0.4→1 opacity) + mild hero parallax (0.25×)
- Hover: Scale + Reveal — image 1.03×, overlay slides up with project location + type
- Signature: Image tilt (3D perspective ±6°) on project cards — cursor-responsive, client:idle, ~3.5KB

**Layout principles:**
- Full-bleed photography sections — images act as "the view"
- Constrained text columns inside full-bleed sections (max 65ch)
- Asymmetric image+text pairs — never centered side-by-side
- Variable section padding: `clamp(5rem, 4rem + 5vw, 12rem)` — never uniform
- 4:5 portrait aspect ratio for project cards

### Design Principles

1. **Slow is luxury.** Every animation, every reveal should be unhurried — 1.2–1.8s minimum for content reveals. The slowness forces visitors to feel before they scan. Price-shoppers will leave. That's the goal.

2. **Photography is the decoration.** The project images carry all visual richness. The typography and palette exist to frame the photography, not compete with it. No background patterns, no decorative SVG shapes, no gradient overlays on body sections.

3. **Warmth over whiteness.** Body background is never `#FFFFFF` or any cool gray. Always warm sand (`#F0E8DC` range minimum). The warmth is the differentiator — every Dutch interior design competitor defaults to cold white.

4. **Space signals quality.** Section padding is generous and variable. Crowded layouts cheapen the work. At the €50K+ price point, breathing room is not wasteful — it's the visual proof that By Shakir does not cut corners.

5. **Qualify before converting.** The site is not trying to convert everyone — it's filtering for the right client. No pricing, no urgency language, no aggressive CTAs. The contact form includes a textarea asking to describe the project. Clients who won't write a paragraph won't be a good fit. This is by design.

### Conversion Rules

- Primary CTA: "Plan een kennismaking" — soft, aspirational
- CTA microcopy: "Vrijblijvend · Persoonlijk contact binnen 24 uur"
- Form: 5 fields, single-step. Phone optional (+275% capture rate vs required). Cloudflare Turnstile + honeypot for spam.
- Gold CTA button: `#C9A962` background + `#2C1F17` text → 7.10:1 AAA ✓
- NEVER: "Versturen" as button text. NEVER: "Neem vandaag nog contact op!" NEVER: "Bel ons maar even."
- Response time signal near every CTA: "Persoonlijk contact binnen 24 uur"

---

### Accessibility Targets

- WCAG AA minimum, AAA where feasible
- Body text (#2C1F17 on #F0E8DC): 13.14:1 AAA ✓
- CTA button (#2C1F17 on #C9A962): 7.10:1 AAA ✓
- Terracotta labels (#8B5230 on #F0E8DC): 5.16:1 AA ✓
- Prefers-reduced-motion: all animations disabled, content visible by default
- Image tilt signature interaction: disabled on touch devices and reduced-motion
